Sleep is when most physical recovery happens, and it shows up in next-day pulse and variability. A run of short or broken nights often nudges resting heart rate up and variability down relative to your baseline. Supporting sleep — a consistent wind-down, limiting late caffeine and screens, a cool dark room — is one of the highest-value wellbeing habits. Framed as encouragement, not instruction.
