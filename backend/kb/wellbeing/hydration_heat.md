care: If you have heart, kidney or blood-pressure conditions, or you are on a low-sodium diet, check with your doctor before adding electrolytes.

Being under-hydrated or warm can nudge pulse upward and make you feel less comfortable. The difference between skin-contact and ambient temperature gives a rough sense of thermal context. When it is warm or a reading drifts from your usual, a light reminder to hydrate and cool down is a reasonable wellbeing nudge. General comfort guidance, not a medical measure.
