title: Staying warm in the cold
source: general wellbeing guidance (original summary)
links: CDC · cold weather & health | https://www.cdc.gov/yellow-book/hcp/environmental-hazards-risks/heat-and-cold-illness-in-travelers.html
care: If you have a heart or circulation condition, be extra cautious with sudden cold exertion, and check with your doctor.

When it is cold outside, layering up before heading out and covering the hands, ears and head — where most heat is lost — helps you stay comfortable. Keeping gently on the move supports circulation and warmth, and warm drinks help too. Easing into any hard outdoor effort is sensible, since cold air can make the same effort feel tougher. This is general comfort guidance, not a medical measure.
