title: Moving regularly and breaking up sitting
source: general wellbeing guidance (original summary)
links: WHO · physical activity & mental health | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK566046/ ;; Review · activity, anxiety & depression | https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10546525/
care: If low or anxious feelings persist, please talk to a professional — movement helps, but it is not a substitute for support.

General wellbeing improves when movement is spread through the day and long unbroken periods of sitting are broken up. Short, frequent movement breaks — standing, a brief walk, some gentle stretching — support circulation, energy, and mood. If a long sedentary stretch is detected, a light prompt to move for a few minutes is a friendly, low-pressure nudge. This is general wellbeing encouragement, not a prescription.
